PERSONALITY TRAITS OF COMMON DOG BREEDS (Plain Text)

Labrador Retriever
- Friendly and outgoing; affectionate, easygoing nature.
- Energetic and playful; needs plenty of exercise (fetch, swimming, vigorous play).
- Highly trainable and eager to please; often excels in obedience and dog sports.
- Versatile working ability; adapts well to service, SAR, hunting, etc.
- Great with families and children; patient and loving.
- Social and generally friendly with other dogs and strangers; not a strong guard dog.

German Shepherd Dog
- Loyal, confident, courageous; strong protective instinct.
- Highly intelligent and trainable; excels in police/service/SAR roles.
- Steady, dependable, calm focus; strong “nerve” when well-bred/socialized.
- Protective and watchful; often aloof with strangers.
- Versatile working ability; happiest with a job/purpose.
- Devoted family companion; gentle and affectionate with their people.

Golden Retriever
- Friendly, gentle, outgoing; famously good-natured.
- Devoted and affectionate; strong desire to please.
- Intelligent and highly trainable; responds well to positive reinforcement.
- Playful and active; keeps a puppy-like spirit into adulthood.
- Patient and sweet with children; great family dog.
- Social with other pets and strangers; not naturally a guard dog.

French Bulldog
- Even-tempered and adaptable; gentle, easygoing.
- Playful and alert; fun-loving without being overly rowdy.
- Affectionate and people-oriented; loves closeness and cuddles.
- Good for apartment living; modest exercise needs.
- Usually quiet; can be a good watchdog (alerts but not aggressive).
- Bright but sometimes stubborn; best with fun, positive training.

Bulldog (English Bulldog)
- Devoted, friendly, gentle companion.
- Easygoing and calm; laid-back temperament.
- Eager to please (though can be stubborn); responds to consistent training.
- Courageous but not aggressive; pacific and dignified.
- Typically good with children and other pets; tolerant.
- Social and adaptable; thrives on human attention.

Cavalier King Charles Spaniel
- Exceptionally affectionate and gentle; loves lap time.
- Friendly and sociable with people and other pets.
- Eager to please; easy to train with gentle methods.
- Playful yet adaptable; happy with walks or couch cuddles.
- Patient with children; kind, non-snappy temperament when treated well.
- Attuned and comforting; often empathetic to owner moods.

Chihuahua
- Bold, confident, fiercely loyal; “big dog” attitude.
- Lively and spirited; enjoys play plus lap time.
- Alert watchdog; often vocal with strangers/noises.
- Wary of strangers initially; benefits from early socialization.
- Affectionate with chosen people; strong bond to 1–2 individuals.
- Independent and strong-willed; can be stubborn.

Cocker Spaniel (American)
- Merry, cheerful, upbeat.
- Gentle and affectionate; loves closeness.
- Friendly and social; generally good with children/other pets.
- Eager to please; responds well to positive, gentle training.
- Sensitive; does best with kind handling (can shut down with harshness).
- Moderate energy; enjoys play and walks, also likes relaxing.

Collie (Rough Collie)
- Devoted and loyal; bonds deeply with family.
- Gentle, patient, sensitive; often excellent with children.
- Intelligent and trainable; responds best to gentle training.
- Watchful/protective in a non-aggressive way; may alert-bark.
- Calm and even-tempered at home; enjoys balanced exercise.
- Dignified yet affectionate; noble, comforting companion.

Dachshund
- Bold and vivacious; big personality.
- Clever and curious; independent and sometimes stubborn.
- Affectionate and loyal; often attaches strongly to a person.
- Fearless and determined; can be reckless if unchecked.
- Playful and spunky; moderate exercise needs.
- Alert and vocal; good little watchdog.

Dalmatian
- Very energetic and athletic; needs lots of exercise.
- Loyal and loving with family.
- Often reserved/aloof with strangers initially.
- Alert and watchful; dependable watchdog.
- Intelligent and strong-willed; needs consistent training.
- Playful and spirited; puppy-like enthusiasm.

Doberman Pinscher
- Fearless, vigilant, self-assured; strong guardian instincts.
- Loyal and affectionate with family; often “velcro” attachment.
- Highly intelligent and trainable; thrives on structured work.
- Confident and composed; steady when well-socialized.
- Protective and often aloof with strangers; discerning.
- Athletic and energetic; needs ample exercise and engagement.

English Springer Spaniel
- Friendly, outgoing, eager to please; very people-oriented.
- Smart and trainable; enjoys working and learning.
- High-spirited and energetic; needs good daily exercise.
- Gentle and affectionate at home; good family dog.
- Alert and will bark to announce visitors; not aggressive.
- Devoted; can be prone to separation anxiety if left alone too much.

German Shorthaired Pointer
- Enthusiastic, high-energy, athletic; thrives on vigorous exercise.
- Intelligent and versatile; excels across many activities.
- Friendly, affectionate, loyal; close family bonds.
- Eager to please; responds well to upbeat training.
- Outgoing and even-tempered; usually good with kids/dogs (socialize early).
- Alert, adventurous, strong prey drive; may chase wildlife.

Great Dane
- Gentle, patient, affectionate; classic “gentle giant.”
- Friendly and sociable; typically amiable with people and animals.
- Trainable and responsive; does best with consistent, gentle training.
- Easygoing and calm indoors; likes lounging.
- Moderate exercise needs; not a high-energy breed.
- Loyal and mildly protective; presence alone is a deterrent.

Greyhound
- Gentle, calm, quiet; polite indoor companion.
- Affectionate and sweet with family; can be reserved.
- Often shy with strangers at first; warms up with trust.
- Low-energy indoors (“couch potato”); enjoys naps.
- Athletic sprinter in short bursts; not a marathon runner.
- Sensitive and non-aggressive; may have prey drive (watch small pets).

Jack Russell Terrier (Parson Russell Terrier)
- Extremely energetic; needs lots of exercise and stimulation.
- Fearless and tenacious; big-dog bravery.
- Clever and inquisitive; strong-willed and can be stubborn.
- Devoted to owners; may be possessive of people/resources.
- Alert and vocal; strong watchdog tendencies.
- Can be dog-selective; intense prey drive.

Mastiff (English Mastiff)
- Gentle, calm, dignified; docile and even-tempered.
- Loyal and protective; composed guardian.
- Courageous and strong-willed; immense strength.
- Affectionate with family; tolerant with children (supervise due to size).
- Independent; needs patient, consistent training.
- Low to moderate energy; content lounging near family.

Pembroke Welsh Corgi
- Spirited, athletic, lively; loves activity.
- Affectionate and companionable; enjoys family life.
- Bold and confident; sometimes bossy/herdy.
- Intelligent and eager to please; quick learner (can be crafty).
- Watchful and vocal; barks to alert.
- Loyal and people-loving; may try to herd kids (nip heels).

Pomeranian
- Vivacious, bold, confident; huge personality.
- Lively and playful; needs stimulation.
- Affectionate and loyal; thrives on human contact.
- Alert and vocal; strong watchdog bark.
- Intelligent but sometimes willful/independent.
- Social and charming; may boss around bigger dogs.

Poodle (Standard/Miniature/Toy)
- Exceptionally intelligent; highly trainable.
- Active and athletic; needs regular exercise.
- Proud/elegant demeanor; affectionate and people-focused.
- Alert; can be a good watchdog (not aggressive).
- Sensitive and emotionally attuned; responds best to positive training.
- Playful and versatile; needs mental stimulation.

Pug
- Charming, affectionate, playful; clownish humor.
- Loves to love and be loved; very people-oriented.
- Even-tempered and outgoing; generally friendly.
- Low to moderate energy; playful spurts plus lots of naps.
- Slightly stubborn but often eager to please; food-motivated.
- Sensitive and intuitive; enjoys close companionship.

Rottweiler
- Confident, calm, courageous; steady temperament.
- Deeply loyal and protective of family.
- Affectionate and gentle with their people when well-socialized.
- Intelligent and trainable; thrives with consistent leadership.
- Reserved/aloof with strangers; discerning.
- Even-tempered and self-controlled; watchful rather than noisy.

Saint Bernard
- Gentle, patient, tolerant; excellent with children.
- Calm and easygoing; laid-back at home.
- Affectionate and devoted; loving family companion.
- Watchful and protective in a mild manner.
- Low to moderate energy; needs only moderate daily activity.
- Amiable with other animals; social and good-natured.

Shetland Sheepdog (Sheltie)
- Very intelligent; highly trainable; loves learning.
- Playful, gentle, devoted; sweet temperament.
- Alert and watchful; often barks to announce changes.
- Sensitive and emotionally attuned; thrives on gentle handling.
- Loyal; can be reserved with strangers; socialization helps.
- Vocal and expressive; “talks” with barks and sounds.

West Highland White Terrier (Westie)
- Bold, confident, spirited; big self-esteem.
- Happy, fun-loving, cheeky; playful antics.
- Affectionate and loyal; also independent.
- Alert and vocal; strong watchdog tendencies.
- Intelligent but stubborn; needs consistent positive training.
- Generally friendly; may be scrappy with other dogs; prey drive for small critters.

Yorkshire Terrier (Yorkie)
- Feisty, brave, sometimes bossy; classic terrier spirit.
- Affectionate and loyal; strong attachment to owners.
- Energetic and playful; lively companion.
- Confident and occasionally stubborn; train with consistency.
- Alert and vocal; excellent little watchdog.
- Social with good socialization; may challenge bigger dogs (supervise).
